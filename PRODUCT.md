# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Roommates or housemates sharing living space who need a quick, transparent way to track who's doing what around the house. They want to avoid arguments about unfinished tasks and see at a glance what's on the plate and who's handling it.

## Product Purpose

A simple, shared chore checklist app for coordinating household tasks. Users create chores, assign them to people, mark them complete, and see the status of everything at once. No complex automation or gamification—just clarity, fairness, and ease. Success means roommates stop arguing about chores and actually finish them.

## Brand Personality

Playful, honest, practical. The app doesn't take itself seriously, but it's genuinely useful. Retro-terminal aesthetic with voltage-yellow accents gives it personality without feeling like a corporate tool. Feels like something friends built for friends, not a SaaS onboarding funnel.

## Anti-references

Not overly corporate or sterile. No business-software vibe, no "streamline your workflow" copy. Not childish or infantile either—this is for adults managing adult responsibilities, not gamified points and streaks.

## Design Principles

1. **Keep it simple.** One chore, one assignee, one status. No hidden complexity.
2. **Show, don't tell.** The board should be self-explanatory at a glance. No tutorials needed.
3. **Be honest about the work.** Chores are unglamorous; the design shouldn't pretend otherwise.
4. **Human first, tech second.** The retro aesthetic is fun, but it serves clarity, not distraction.
5. **Fairness is the feature.** Everyone can see who's carrying the load. Transparency builds trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA standard (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text). Support for reduced motion (no excessive animations). Color is not the only signal of status (always pair color with text or icons). The retro monospace aesthetic is visually distinctive; ensure text remains readable at all sizes.
